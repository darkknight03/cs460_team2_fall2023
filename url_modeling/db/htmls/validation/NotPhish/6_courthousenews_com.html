Rulings

by Kelsey Jukam

Trump train

A federal court in Texas refused to dismiss a lawsuit arising from a 2020 incident in which supporters of then-President Donald Trump surrounded a Biden campaign bus on the highway between San Antonio and Austin, Texas. The plaintiffs, including former state Senator Wendy Davis, claim the defendants and others came within inches of the bus and one vehicle slammed into a campaign staffer’s car.

Attorney fees

The Illinois Supreme Court dismissed Chicago Alderman Byron Sigcho-Lopez’s suit alleging his predecessor Daniel Solis’ campaign committee illegally paid personal legal fees from campaign funds. The funds went to a firm Solis retained when he began cooperating with an FBI in its investigation into corruption by Illinois public officials.

Weight loss drug

A federal court in Florida ruled a couple can pursue their negligence and products liability claims based on a defective design theory against pharmaceutical companies Eisai and Arena Pharmaceuticals. Mary Milana claims the drug “Belviq” caused her to develop breast cancer. The plaintiffs have plausibly alleged the drug was defective because its active ingredient tended to cause cancer in clinical trials.

Police shooting

The Ninth Circuit upheld the denial of qualified immunity sought by Riverside County Sheriff’s Department Sergeant Dan Ponder in a suit alleging he used excessive force when he shot Clemente Najera-Aguirre six times without warning, killing him.

Klamath River project

A federal court in California declined to dismiss the U.S. Bureau of Reclamation’s crossclaim in a suit over its biological opinion on a Klamath River project. The bureau has adequately alleged a conflict between an Oregon Water Resources Department order preventing it from releasing water classified as stored at the Upper Klamath Lake and its obligations under the Endangered Species Act.
