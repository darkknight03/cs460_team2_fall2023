Risk of losses for Russian bondholders remains high despite recent coupon payments
More on the Russia-Ukraine crisis
22 Mar 2022|Moody's Investors Service

Although some holders of Russian eurobonds reported that they had recently received coupon payments in US dollars, the risk of default and investor losses remains elevated because of the marked deterioration in the government's ability and willingness to meet its debt obligations in recent weeks. Furthermore, the expiry of a general license allowing for these payments is likely to further impair investors' ability to receive foreign-currency debt repayments.

CBDCs can promote financial inclusion, stability; success depends on design, institutional credibility
22 Mar 2022|Moody's Investors Service

Emerging markets are increasingly considering implementing central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) as an alternative to cryptocurrencies and crypto assets, which are volatile and can threaten financial stability. However, even if still-nascent implementation efforts accelerate over the next year, it will take years before CBDCs become a key feature of the global financial architecture.

Scenario analysis: Potential Russia-NATO military conflict would have swift and severe credit impact
More on the Russia-Ukraine crisis
23 Mar 2022|Moody's Investors Service

A hypothetical military conflict between Russia and NATO countries would likely lead to rapid credit transitions for many more debt issuers than if the current crisis remains limited to Russia and Ukraine. A potential Russia-NATO military conflict is not our baseline expectation but an alternative downside scenario.
