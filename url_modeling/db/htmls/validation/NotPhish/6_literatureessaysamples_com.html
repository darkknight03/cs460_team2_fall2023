Literature Essay Structure

Writing the literary essay starts with the thesis. This is the point at which you reflect the general idea about the writing you’re going to produce. It’s the primary argument that gives the essay direction limiting it from being just a bag of different sentences. Often, you’ll be given a prompt in the essay task. It simplifies the process of creating the thesis because you have to create one with an explanation of why you think so, not just yes or no.The next step would be to write a title and introduction. This is like a gateway to the whole work you’re going to produce in the next several days. The title must evidently indicate what is the focus of the analysis in the literary essay. It should contain the author’s name and text that’s going to be analyzed. It must be informative and concise at the same time. A good approach would be to use a quote from the literary piece followed by a colon with explanation, name of the author, and title. The introduction is the next part that provides a brief overview of the upcoming argument in your work. It includes the thesis statement in the beginning, summary of the whole essay in the middle, and opening with a related idea that narrows down to the end of the intro. You’ll start the introduction with a general statement that reflects on who is the author and what’s the role of this text in the context of literature and human history. You might also talk about the popular idea about this text in the mass culture, later showing how your thesis contradicts it entirely. In the middle, there is the technique called signposting you can use. It helps to mark what’s going to happen in the main body of the essay.The body of the essay should be placed between introduction and conclusion. Literary analysis essay structure is similar to many other types of writing. It usually consists of five paragraphs: intro, three body paragraphs, and conclusion. Each of the body paragraphs must focus on a single topic. For example, in one paragraph you review characters and plot, and in the other –  literary devices used by the author. Each body paragraph should begin with a topic sentence that keeps the points gathered around a single idea. Good topic sentences allow readers to understand the whole premise of the paragraph, introduce new arguments, and connect to other paragraphs. Your conclusion shouldn’t introduce any new quotes or evidence from the literary work. It’s purpose is to wrap up the essay in general, summarizing the findings that you had. You need to  emphasize how significant they are to understand the reading from the perspective of your thesis. Conclusion is basically a reversed introduction. You begin with supporting your thesis based on the findings from the body, later moving to open questions about other possible meanings hidden by the author. 

How to Choose the Best Literature Essay Topic

The choice of the literary essay topic should be strong and relevant to the piece of literature. There are tons of topics that colleges and universities offer for students to choose from. Another scenario is when students come up with their own topics and specific essay types. It has to be both original enough and not too narrow to give you space for developing a solid narrative. You’ll have to choose the topic according to the type of literary essay you’re going to write. There are narrative, argumentative, compare and contrast, expository, and other types of essays that fit into the subject of literature. Those types of essays have different requirements, so the final name for the topic will be chosen according to the specifications. One of the strategies that you can follow is to choose three good topics that fit your essay type. Do the quick research on the supplementary materials that will help you write it. If you see that there aren’t enough sources around the web or on your hands that will help you to render a good paper in optimal time, then move to the next topic. If you see that the topic appealing to you is too broad, make it more specific by narrowing the idea expressed in it. All that is left is to choose the strongest one that is easy to approach from several perspectives, available sources and their preciseness. All in all, the topic that you select must be interesting for you to write about. The whole creation process will be a breeze if the topic fascinates you. Besides, you’ll be able to think creatively when writing it. Right questions will pop up in your head as quickly as you’d find answers to them. Interesting topic is about to inspire you and give the boost of energy to complete the assignment on time and with powers to edit and polish it afterwards.
