History remembers the 1911 Triangle Shirtwaist Company factory fire in New York City as one of the most infamous American industrial incidents. A fire broke out in the factory on March 25, 1911, and killed 146 workers inside, mostly young female immigrants. The tragic event brought much-needed attention to the dangerous sweatshop conditions in factories as many of those deaths could've been avoided if not for neglected safety features and locked doors.
