03/10/2022

Research Assessment

How research data deliver non-academic impacts: A secondary analysis of UK Research Excellence Framework impact case studies

Using a secondary analysis of high-scoring impact case studies, Jensen and colleagues explore the non-academic impacts of research data and report that intervention methods such as curation, analysis, or product development are needed to faciliate broad non-academic impact. 

Image credit: Marketing cyfrowy technologia zeszyt by PhotoMIX-Company, Pixabay License

03/11/2022

Oncology

Upregulation of PODXL and ITGB1 in pancreatic cancer tissues preoperatively obtained by EUS-FNAB correlates with unfavorable prognosis of postoperative pancreatic cancer patients

Taniuchi K. and colleagues investigate how the upregulation of PODXL and ITGB1 in surgically resected pancreatic cancer tissues is correlated with an unfavorable postoperative prognosis and describe a preoperative neoadjuvant therapy for pancreatic cancer patients.

Image credit: Pancreas Organ by zachvanstone8, Pixabay License

03/14/2022

Computational Biology

Large-scale design and refinement of stable proteins using sequence-only models

Singer and colleagues designed neural network models that enable high-speed, automated creation of stable protein designs on a large scale, using only primary sequences.

Image credit: Fig 1 by Singer et al., CC BY 4.0

03/04/2022

Space Technology

SISPO: Space Imaging Simulator for Proximity Operations

Pajusalu and colleagues develop a simulator that can be used for imaging and planning deep space mission scenarios, and illustrate its performance for examples such as small solar system bodies and spacecraft fly-bys.

Image credit: Fig 13 by Pajusalu et al., CC BY 4.0

03/18/2022

everyone blog

An Interview with Biogeochemist Alex Cory

In this interview, we chat with Alex about her recent publication in PLOS ONE and the important role that peatlands play in sequestering CO2. We also learn about life as an early career researcher, and the NSF-funded EMERGE program to increase diversity in STEM.

Image credit: Stordalen (Kjerag) by Lightsh4dow

03/01/2022

Everyone blog

An Interview with Paleoclimate Modeler Hu Yang

Here, we chat with Dr. Hu Yang about his recent publication in PLOS ONE and his predictions of the future of the Greenland Ice Sheet – the second largest body of ice on Earth, which has the potential to dramatically raise global sea level.

Image credit: NASA Goddard Photo and Video

02/25/2022

Everyone blog

Physical Oceanography - a chat with the Guest Editors of our upcoming collection

PLOS ONE has an open Call for Papers on Physical Oceanography, for which selected publications will be showcased in a special collection. Here, we chat with some of the Guest Editors to learn more about their research and how advances in this field can provide a better understanding of future environmental change.

Image credit: NASA Goddard Photo and Video

03/10/2022

Marine Biology

Characterization of fin whale song off the Western Antarctic Peninsula

Širović and Wood provide valuable new data on long term evolution of the fin whale song of the Western Antartic Peninsula.

Image credit: Fin Whale feeding by U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration et al., Public Domain

03/10/2022

Molecular Modeling

Automated fitting of transition state force fields for biomolecular simulations

Quinn and colleagues present a new workflow for enzyme simulations that combines quantum-guided molecular mechanics, transition state force fields and machine learning.

Image credit: Fig 1 by Quinn et al., CC BY 4.0

03/09/2022

Psychology

A calming hug: Design and validation of a tactile aid to ease anxiety

Haynes and colleagues present a novel huggable haptic interface that pneumatically simulates slow breathing for anxiety relief, and demonstrate efficacy in reducing induced anxiety.

Image credit: Fig 3 by Haynes et al., CC BY 4.0

03/14/2022

Cardiovascular Medicine

Pacemaker translocations and power laws in cardiomyocyte cultures

Dunham et al. study pacemaker translocations in hPSC-derived cardiomyocyte culture and power law analysis by microelectrode arrays and modeling. 

Image credit: Cardiac Pulse by OpenClipart-Vectors, Pixabay License
