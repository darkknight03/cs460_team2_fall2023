CQC’s Out of Sight progress update shows worrying lack of improvement on use of restraint, segregation and seclusion

Today’s progress update by the Care Quality Commission (CQC) on their October 2020 Out of Sight review into the use of restraint, segregation and seclusion has revealed little progress in the reduction of these practices in mental health settings.
  • Thursday, 24 March 2022

    Covid Inquiry risks missing the mark if it omits mental health

    Mind will apply to become a “core participant” of the Covid inquiry to make sure young people’s mental health is at the centre of its aims, and for children and young people to receive the adequate mental health support.
  • Wednesday, 23 March 2022

    Chancellor fails to stop benefit claimants facing second drop in income in under six months

    Mind recently joined 58 other charities calling for an increase to benefits in line with inflation, in response to the cost of living crisis. Unfortunately in today’s Spring Statement the Chancellor of the Exchequer failed to announce such a rise. This follows on from the £20-a-week cut to Universal Credit in October 2021.
