Description

CoinJar: buy, sell and spend Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies with Australia’s best cryptocurrency app.


CoinJar is Australia’s longest-running cryptocurrency exchange. The CoinJar app lets you buy and sell cryptocurrencies using Australian dollars simply, securely and with low fees.


Using CoinJar, you can:


BUY & SELL CRYPTOCURRENCY

Trade between Australian dollars and over 50 supported cryptocurrencies, including Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), Shiba Inu (SHIB), Litecoin (LTC), Ripple (XRP), Stellar Lumens (XLM), Algorand (ALGO), USD Coin (USDC), Tether (USDT), TerraUSD (UST), Polygon/Matic (MATIC), 0x Token (ZRX), OMG Network (OMG), Compound (COMP), ChainLink Token (LINK), yearn.finance (YFI), Uniswap (UNI), Maker (MKR), Aave (AAVE), Bancor Network Token (BNT), Curve DAO (CRV), Synthetix Network Token (SNX), SushiSwap (SUSHI), Balancer (BAL), Enjin (ENJ), The Graph (GRT), Axie Infinity (AXS), Fantom (FTM), Wrapped Bitcoin (WBTC) and many others.


BUY CRYPTOCURRENCY INSTANTLY WITH CREDIT/DEBIT CARD^

Instantly purchase crypto using your Visa or Mastercard with only a 2% fee – some of the cheapest credit card purchases in Australia or the world.


DEPOSIT CASH FREE AND FAST

0% fees when depositing with PayID or BPAY. Cash in-person also available. PayID deposits are almost instantaneous and should arrive in minutes.*


SPEND YOUR CRYPTOCURRENCY WITH COINJAR CARD

Spend your crypto like cash with your free CoinJar Card, a pre-paid crypto Mastercard. Available as both a physical and digital card, with Apple Pay functionality.


CONVERT YOUR CRYPTO

Swap any cryptocurrency for another with the click of a button.


QUICK CASH WITHDRAWALS

Withdraw funds directly to your Australian bank accounts for free. Withdrawals are almost instantaneous and should arrive in minutes.


SEND & RECEIVE

Send crypto and receive payments using a QR code, wallet address or CoinJar username.


COINJAR BUNDLES & RECURRING BUYS

CoinJar Bundles let you purchase collections of popular cryptocurrencies, allowing you to diversify your portfolio in one simple transaction.


Each bundle’s performance is automatically tracked, and can be grown by adding more funds or by setting up Recurring Buys, direct from your bank account.


EASY TAX REPORTING

View all past transactions and receipts, with conversion rate, status, date and time. Download comprehensive transaction reports to make tax time a breeze.


SET PRICE ALERTS

Get daily price notifications and set price alerts so you never miss a trading opportunity.


SECURITY FIRST

Set two-factor authentication via passcode or Touch/Face ID for login and transactions. Lock your account remotely if you lose your device.


CoinJar follows industry-leading security protocols. More than 90% of all assets are stored offline in geographically redundant secure locations. We maintain full reserves at all times and user assets are fully insured through BitGo Custody.


FRIENDLY SUPPORT


Our local and in-house Support team is available close to 24/7 and is just an email away at support@coinjar.com.


Follow us on Twitter @GetCoinJar for news and updates.

X45 , 13/11/2021

Solid iPhone app, absent iPad version

The CoinJar iPhone app covers most of the at-a-glance account features you might need, in a user-friendly and design-conscious way. It is straightforward to go between the CoinJar website and app with a consistent iconography and layout.


Disappointing for many though will be the necessity to use the iPhone version on iPad. With the latest tools, iOS developers have never had it this easy to develop their apps for multiple devices and screen sizes, and the reasons not to do so are quickly vanishing. Unfortunately, if you’re relaxing on the couch with your iPad, phone at a distance, navigating the higher security of a CoinJar website login is currently the only alternative.


It has also been more than a year now that iPhone app users have been greeted with a “Coming Soon” message when tapping on the CoinJar Exchange button. A promise never delivered upon is always harder to accept than no promise at all. It’s fine that such complex functionality hasn’t arrived yet, just so long as we’re not encouraged to anticipate it.
