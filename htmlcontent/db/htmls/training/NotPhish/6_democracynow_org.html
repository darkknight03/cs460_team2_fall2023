ColumnMar 24, 2022

Judge Ketanji Brown Jackson and the Slowly Bending Arc of Justice

By Amy Goodman & Denis Moynihan
Harriet Tubmans birth date is unknown, but some estimate it as March, 1822 exactly 200 years before Judge Jacksons historic Supreme Court confirmation hearing, and just over 200 years after the first Africans were sold into slavery in North America in 1619. The arc, 400 years long, is bending towards justice.
