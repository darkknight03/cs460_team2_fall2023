Evan Israel ’24 steps onto the ice, takes a deep breath & waits for the music to play to begin her routine. But unlike individual figure skating, she’s not alone. As the music starts, she moves in perfect synchronization with 11 other ice skaters. As she twists through intricate spins she catches a glimpse of Sami Winawer ’23, not only a team member, but her Brandeis classmate.

Synchronized figure skating is not a club sport at Brandeis, but with the help of Evan and Sami, it may become a part of a future Winter Olympics.

The sport is not what spectators are used to seeing from figure skating. Synchronized figure skating involves 8-16 athletes skating precisely synced, in choreographed routines of spins, formations, lifts & intricate movements.

Due to the cost for each skater on the team to travel, the sport hasn’t been featured in the Olympics. Evan & Sami are members of Hayden Select, a team in the new Senior Elite 12 division looking to change that.

The team has notched some significant successes lately, scoring a gold medal in France during the Synchronized Skating French Cup. “We get to say that we competed in the first Elite 12 division and became the first champions,” said Evan. “We are making history.”

In addition to their Brandeis friends attending their exhibitions & cheering them on, the two are encouraged by their professors as well. “Our professors have been really supportive of our skating,” said Sami. “Whenever I tell them they are excited to learn more about it.”

While Sami & Evan may not get the chance to compete in the Olympics, they hope that their team will impact the future of the sport.

“I’ll hopefully be in law school by then,” said Sami. “but while watching from home I'll be really proud that I helped pave that path.”

This week, you may have seen some new classmates: 🦸‍♂️, 👩🏿‍⚖️ or maybe even a 🐮?

You don't need to get your eye prescription checked, your costumed classmates were members of the Jewish community & friends celebrating Purim! Purim celebrates victory over oppression, & is recognized by retelling the story of Esther (the Megillah), sharing gifts of food and drink (called mishloach manot) & dressing up.

Both @m54chabad/@chababealert & @brandeis_hillel celebrated through events across campus that were open to the entire community regardless of faith observance. Chabad handed out mishloach manot/Purim boxes on Rabb Steps & Hillel held its annual Brandeis Breakfast Club in Levin Ballroom in the morning (both pictured here).
