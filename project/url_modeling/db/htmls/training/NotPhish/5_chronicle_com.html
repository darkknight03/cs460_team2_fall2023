Virtual Events

  • Explorations of Active Learning

    UPCOMING: March 28 at 2 p.m., ET.
    Little more than two years after faculty members adjusted their teaching styles to suit the needs of remote learning, many are once again being asked to rethink the way they teach in favor of an active-learning approach. How can college leaders encourage them to embrace it as campuses return to more normal operations? A panel of experts examines the issue in “Explorations of Active Learning,” a virtual forum on what college leaders need to know. With Support From Adobe. Register here.

  • The Changing Campus Workplace: What Presidents and Provosts Need to Know

    UPCOMING: March 29 at 2 p.m., ET.
    The pandemic quickly transformed the higher-ed workplace to a largely remote or hybrid environment. A panel of experts convenes at a virtual forum to discuss how to manage a remote work force, and what policy changes are worth considering. With Support From ServiceNow. Register here.

  • Closing the Equity Gap in Student Success

    UPCOMING: March 30 at 2 p.m., ET.
    Amid persisting inequalities laid bare by the pandemic, colleges are placing renewed focus on closing the equity gap in student success. In a virtual forum, “Closing the Equity Gap in Student Success,” a panel of experts will discuss how colleges are doubling down on best practices to help students of color, as well as those from low-income and first-generation households, thrive and reach their goals. With Support From Florida Atlantic University. Register here.

  • How Do You Distinguish Yourself Through Your Curriculum?

    UPCOMING: March 31 at 2 p.m., ET.
    As colleges seek to set themselves apart from the competition, many are taking a second look at their curricular offerings. Though curricular change is rarely easy, it can be done. This virtual forum discusses how colleges have carried out those changes, and offers tips for other institutions looking to do the same. With Support From Coursedog. Register here.
