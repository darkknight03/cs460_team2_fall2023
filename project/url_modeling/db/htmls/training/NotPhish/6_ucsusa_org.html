De-Escalate Nuclear Tensions

Urge President Biden to pursue diplomacy and policy decisions that avert the risk of nuclear conflict in Ukraine.

Tell President Biden to de-escalate nuclear tensions

The Russian government's invasion of Ukraine and an escalated, prolonged conflict could harm thousands and displace millions. Any conflict is unpredictable, but the involvement of nuclear-armed states raises the stakes. The possibility it could escalate to nuclear war – through a misunderstanding, miscalculation, or poor judgement – is real.

This moment requires the United States to pursue diplomacy and policy decisions that de-escalate tensions and avert the risk of nuclear conflict. Urge President Biden to take stabilizing steps that would communicate to Russia and the world that the United States is unwilling to fight a nuclear war.

Dear [elected official],

Your administration’s Nuclear Posture Review is an opportunity to take stabilizing steps that would communicate to Russia and the world that the United States is unwilling to fight a nuclear war. I urge you to follow the advice of scientists and nuclear weapons experts by declaring a “no first use” nuclear policy, stating the United States will not use nuclear weapons first, and end the president’s sole authority to order a nuclear strike.
