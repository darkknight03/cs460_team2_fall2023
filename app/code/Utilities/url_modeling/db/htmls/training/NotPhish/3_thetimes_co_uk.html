Nature notes

The shady base of the hedge is a mass of purple, with sweet violets and lesser periwinkle gleaming dark and light. The easiest way to distinguish sweet violets from their cousin the dog violet is by crouching and taking a sniff: the two look extremely similar, but dog violets usually flower a little later in the year and smell of nothing at all. Sweet violets smell attracts early pollinators, and its nectar is an important food source at the start of the season. The periwinkle, meanwhile, is most likely a garden escape, although some botanists believe it may in fact be native, at least in the southeast.
